How Remote Work is Changing Lives of Women in Tier 2 Cities

The remote work revolution isn't just a metro phenomenon. Across Jaipur, Lucknow, Indore and hundreds of smaller towns, women are rewriting their stories — one work from home opportunity at a time.

TheBirbal Team
Published January 28, 2025

For decades, career opportunities for women in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities of India have been limited by geography, culture, and infrastructure. Remote work is changing all of that — and the transformation is nothing short of remarkable.

At TheBirbal, we work with over 500 women professionals across 15+ cities in India. A significant portion of them are based not in Delhi or Mumbai, but in cities like Jaipur, Lucknow, Bhopal, Indore, Nagpur and Patna. Their stories are inspiring — and they're becoming more common every day.

The Old Barrier: Geography Was Destiny

Until recently, a woman in Lucknow with excellent communication skills and 5 years of experience had very limited options. Local jobs often paid a fraction of what metro companies offered. Relocation meant leaving family behind — an option many couldn't or wouldn't take.

The result? Millions of talented women were effectively locked out of the best opportunities their skills deserved. Remote work broke that barrier entirely.

What's Changing — Real Numbers

According to our internal data at TheBirbal, women from Tier 2 cities who join our platform see an average income increase of 40-60% compared to local employment options. More importantly, they retain full flexibility over their schedule.

TheBirbal Insight: 68% of our active women professionals are based outside the top 8 metros of India — and they consistently rank among our highest performers.

Three Women, Three Cities, Three Stories

Priya, Jaipur: A mother of two with a commerce degree, Priya had been out of the workforce for 4 years. She joined TheBirbal as a customer support specialist and within 6 months was leading a team of 8. "I never thought I'd have a corporate career while being home for my children," she says.

Rekha, Lucknow: Rekha was working at a local BPO for ₹8,000/month. After joining TheBirbal's sales support vertical, she now earns ₹22,000 per month working flexible hours. "The training was excellent and the team was always supportive," she shares.

Sunita, Nagpur: A graduate with no prior work experience, Sunita was skeptical. "I thought these online jobs were not real." After 8 months with TheBirbal, she handles recruitment coordination for three partner companies and has been promoted twice.

Why Tier 2 Women Are Actually Better Suited for WFH

This might surprise some, but our experience shows that women from Tier 2 cities often outperform their metro counterparts in remote work settings. Here's why:

They have lower distraction levels, stronger family support structures, higher motivation to prove themselves, and a deep hunger for career growth that hasn't been dimmed by years of corporate routine. They bring freshness, reliability and commitment that is rare.

Remote work doesn't just give women a job — it gives them their confidence back.

What TheBirbal Does Differently

Most platforms that claim to offer WFH jobs don't invest in the women they place. TheBirbal does things differently. Every woman who joins our platform goes through structured onboarding, product and communication training, and ongoing mentorship.

We also build community — WhatsApp groups, monthly virtual meets, recognition programs and peer learning sessions. Because we know that isolation is the biggest challenge of remote work, and a connected community solves it.
